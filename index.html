Our Oceans Need Help

Plastic

90% of single-use plastic isn’t recycled. Plastic is a large pollutant of the earth's oceans and plastic filled oceans are unsafe for marine life. Many animals are found with plastic in their bodies, as a cause of death.

Climate Change

Our ocean's temperatures are rising, which has extremely negative affects on marine life. Greenhouse gases are regulated, and nutrients are recycled by the ecosystems in our oceans (WWF 2018). With rising sea levels and temperatures, these processes are imbalanced, negatively effecting the health of natural systems.

Over Fishing

Overfishing is a major threat to our oceans. At least two-thirds of large fish have been taken from our oceans, causing a break down of 33% of fish populations. There is more fishing than fish in our seas.

Ocean Harming Products

Products made from ingredients naturally found in the ocean harm the ocean's ecosystems. Some makeup contains marine animal products, some jewellery is made from marine animals, and chemicals that you wash down your drains often end up in the ocean.

The Facts

Our planet is dependant on the ocean. The ocean supplies half of the earth’s oxygen and 72% of our world is covered by the ocean.

Almost 3 billion people rely on fish for their main source of protein, if fish don't have a healthy habitat to live in, these people will lose a large amount of their diet.

The ocean helps to regulate the world's climate by trapping heat, saving our oceans will help save our planet.

The ocean has a large biodiversity, and is the home of the world's largest animal, and smallest bacteria.

Many everyday cosmetic and health products use "Squalene", derived from sharks, who are often killed for this ingredient.

Everyday products contain chemicals that are washed down drains, when you wash your hands, or have a shower. These chemicals can be extremely harmful to the ocean. Even some sunscreens contain chemicals which are dangerous to the ocean.

The ocean has given human's the ability to create medical advances, by supplying ingredients used for fighting Alzheimer's disease, cancer, arthritis, and heart disease.

The ocean not only regualtes our planet's temperature, but also helps to regulate our weather.

Water conservation is a large part of helping our oceans, and our planet. Fresh water is a limited source, used in everyday life for manufacturing, food, drinking, and recreational use.
Some easy ways you can conserve water include:
- Taking shorter showers
- Turn the water off when brushing your teeth
- Only use your washing machine on a full load
- Water your garden in cooler parts of the day

Water Corporation have more information on how you can conserve water.

Tonnes of waste ends up in our oceans every year, as people aren't aware of the products they use, and how everyday practices may harm the ocean. Some ways that you can be more mindful of your waste include:
- Recycle rubbish that is recyclable
- Be aware of the packaging of products you buy
- Compost food waste and scraps
Recycling Near You is a website that helps you find a facility near you where you can recycle, and what materials are eligible to be recycled.

At least 8 million tonnes of plastic end up in the ocean each year, and many animals eat this plastic causing death and endagering species. You can help these species survive, and the ocean flourish by following simple rules like the following:
- Use re-usable bottles and straws
- Use re-usable bags for shopping
- Read if packaging is recyclable before you purchase a product
For more information, National Geographic is a great resource.
